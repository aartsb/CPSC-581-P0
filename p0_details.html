Video Demonstration

This project is based on showcasing a piece of work that would delight my client and fellow classmate, Candy.

  • For this mini movie, I used Adobe Sketch to sketch each scene.
  • After reiterating on the sketches, I used FlipAclip and iMovie to make the movie. Adding soundeffects, voiceover and some edits brought the movie to life in the real sense!

    Sketches that showcase my brainstorming session before I landed on the final movie idea.

  • Idea 1:

    • A 'gif' idea which is broken down in some phases in order to show the intended transitions.

  • Idea 2:

    • A stop-watch idea which stops at a random time on every other button action such that it spits out "CHOCOLATE TIME!" message.

  • Idea 3:

    • A piano that is being played by my client, Candy and her father, such that upon the button action, a random note is played and using animation the notes sway across the page till the note is playing.

  • Idea 4

    • A caricature of Candy, first eating chocolates from her right hand, then that from the left hand. This happens in a loop, created by animation.

  • Idea 5:

    • Expressing the love for her family and chocolates, this woud translate into a fun animation of Candy flinging am ice-cream towards her sister.

  • Idea 6:

    • A flipbook showing an instance of Candy entering a self-serve ice-cream shopping and pouring some ice-cream for herself.

  • Idea 7:

    • As the title suggests, a Candy Shooter, which upon the single button action would shoot candies endlessly. Now how delightful is that?

  • Idea 8:

    • It's raining candies, whilst Candy is twirling in her beautiful flowy dress, enjoying the candies falling right into her mouth.

  • Idea 9:

    • BEWARE! A TUG OF CHOCO WAR! But this is exactly what Candy would strive to win. Why so? Well, first it's called TUG OF CHOCO WAR and secondly, the tug is a sac of chocolates which will be hurled towards the winner.

  • Idea 10:

    • Now, for the idea that I picked comprises the flipbook idea with that of the 'gif', a mini movie created by sketches (created by yours truly!) with each sketch showing the story progression. I finalised this idea as it felt most organic to the interview with Candy where she got closer to her family during COVID-19, while also the importance of chocolates that kept her going.

    So does this change after the pandemic ends? Lets that a look!
